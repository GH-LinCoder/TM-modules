System Philosophy

From abstract thoughts to data base tables

We have three categories called Persons, Tracking, and Bridging.

'Persons' have as their core data structure the 'profile'. 'Tracking' has as its core data structure 'tasks' and the linked sub-structure 'steps'. Bridging has as its core data structure 'task_assignments'.

So on one side we have persons, on the other side we have tracking and what links them is bridging.

Persons are represented by profiles. When a person gets bridged to tracking they are linked. This is when a 'profile' is linked via 'task_assignments' to a 'task'.

In the process of assignment we bring into existence a category 'student' we may also bring into existence the category 'manager'. These are fleeting categories.

Inside 'tracking' we have two closely related data types 'tasks' and 'steps'. Steps are a sub category of tasks. Steps cannot exist alone. Tasks always have at least 3 steps.
